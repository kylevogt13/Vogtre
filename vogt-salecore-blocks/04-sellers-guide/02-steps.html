1

Understand Why You're Selling

Each listing differs based on seller needs. We listen to what matters most and prepare a customized action plan.

2

Determine Selling Price, Disclosures & Listing Agreement

Proper pricing generates maximum buyer interest. Overpricing can lead to reduced interest and you lose peak marketing time.

3

Start Preparations

Pre-open title/escrow to uncover title problems early. Pre-listing inspections prevent unexpected surprises. Home staging: declutter, depersonalize, repair, deep clean.

4

Develop a Marketing Strategy

Drive the right people to your listing through social media, agent-to-agent referrals, traditional media, and SEO advertising.

5

Evaluate Offers

Evaluate each offer and confirm buyer pre-approval. Review purchase price, deposit, down payment, financing, contingencies, and settlement date.

6

Accept an Offer

Once both parties agree to terms, the purchase contract is signed.

7

During Escrow

Buyers conduct inspections and lenders order appraisals. Buyers acknowledge disclosures and remove contingencies.

8

Closing

Ownership transfers to the buyer. Arrange utility cancellations and provide keys, remotes, and smart device instructions.
